Local Specialists

Belfast's condensation & mould experts

Belfast's older housing stock — the red-brick Victorian and Edwardian terraces and "kitchen houses" found across East, South, North and West Belfast — was never designed for the way we live today. Solid walls, retrofitted double glazing, and draught-proofing trap moisture indoors, and the result is streaming windows and black mould every winter.

We install positive input ventilation (PIV) throughout Belfast and the BT1–BT17 postcodes, from Ballyhackamore and Cregagh in the east to the Lisburn Road, Malone and Ormeau in the south, the Antrim Road and Cavehill in the north, and Andersonstown and the Falls in the west. Student HMOs around Queen's and the Holylands — where moisture loads are especially high — are a particular speciality.

A single quiet unit, fitted in around two hours, gently replaces damp stale air with fresh filtered air across the whole home. Most Belfast customers see windows running dry within a week and mould gone within weeks.
