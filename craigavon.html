Curing condensation across Craigavon

The Craigavon area — taking in the new-town district itself along with the larger towns of Lurgan and Portadown — was famously built around a pair of artificial lakes. Combined with the low-lying ground near the River Bann and Lough Neagh, that means homes here are especially prone to high humidity, condensation and persistent black mould.

We install positive input ventilation (PIV) throughout Craigavon and the BT62–BT67 postcodes, covering Lurgan, Portadown, Brownlow, Bleary, Waringstown, Gilford and Tandragee. From the area's many estate homes to older terraces in the town centres, PIV delivers a permanent, low-cost cure.

Fitted in around two hours with no mess, PIV gently replaces damp stale air with fresh filtered air. Most Craigavon customers see windows running dry within a week.
